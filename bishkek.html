The Golden City

Nestled at the foot of the celestial Tian Shan mountains, Bishkek emerges like a mirage of gold and emerald—a city where Soviet grandeur dances with nomadic spirit under endless Central Asian skies.

Founded as a caravan stop on the Silk Road, Bishkek has transformed into a metropolis where time flows differently. Here, the air shimmers with the scent of samsa baking in clay ovens, while the shadows of mighty Soviet monuments stretch across manicured parks.

By day, the city dazzles with wide boulevards lined with chestnut trees. By night, the glow of chaikhanas (tea houses) illuminates secret courtyards where poets, merchants, and travelers exchange stories that will become tomorrow's legends.

Nomadic Soul

Tea Ceremonies

In chaikhanas hidden like treasures, the pouring of tea becomes meditation, and porcelain bowls hold reflections of mountain and cloud.

🎪

World Nomad Games

When warriors on horseback revive ancient sports, their cries echo from Bishkek across the Eurasian steppe in a celebration of nomadic heritage.

The world's longest epic poem lives in the voices of storytellers who can recite its 500,000 lines—a verbal tapestry woven through centuries.
